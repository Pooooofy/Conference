About the Conference

The University of Rizal System deems it essential to sustain its efforts on the internationalization of operations. The 2024 URS International Multidisciplinary Research and Extension Advocacy Conference for Champions of Change (IMREACH) intends to serve as an avenue for researchers and extension practitioners across disciplines to share relevant information, research outputs, and best practices in community development.

This will benefit the interested groups with enhanced understanding and appreciation of research development and extension services not only as institutional mandates but as commitment to help improve life. The conference will provide opportunities to strengthen partnerships and gain knowledge on innovative and scaled-up initiatives for more empowered communities.

In Particular, This Conference Will:

  • Highlight the significance of scaled-up efforts in the internationalization of research and extension services.
  • Provide a platform for service learning through the presentation of qualified papers in research and extension.
  • Address global sustainability challenges with research and extension for a more progressive society.
  • Promote inter/multi-disciplinary approaches in research and extension undertakings.

Research Category

Call for Abstracts

Thematic Areas

  • Group 1: Quality Education
  • Group 2: Good Health and Well-Being, Gender Equality
  • Group 3: Sustainable Communities and Cities, Climate Action
  • Group 4: Responsible Consumption and Production, Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure

Abstract Format

The abstract should be a 150-300 word informative digest that summarizes the objectives, methodology, and significant findings of your paper. The abstract must be single-spaced with 3-5 keywords. Abstracts should be submitted via the provided link. Authors will be notified if their abstracts are accepted for presentation.

Research Paper Report Format

The full research paper should be formatted as follows:

  • Font: Arial, Size: 11pt
  • Spacing: Double-spaced
  • Margins: 25.4 cm (1 inch) on all sides
  • The paper should include: Title, Authors, Abstract (max 300 words), Introduction, Objectives, Methodology, Results, and References

Paper Presentation Format

Each presentation will be allocated ten minutes for pre-recorded presentations, followed by a ten-minute Q&A session. The following format is recommended for the presentation:

  • Title: Include the project title, proponents, duration, budget, and implementing agency
  • Introduction: Provide an overview of the research
  • Objectives: List the objectives in bullet form
  • Conceptual Framework: Provide a clear framework for your research
  • Methodology: Break it down by objective
  • Results and Discussions: Present key findings in bullet points
  • Implications and Recommendations: List the implications and recommendations in bullet form

Extension Category

Call for Abstract

Thematic Areas:

  • Group 1: Poverty Alleviation, Food Production and Safety
  • Group 2: Sustainable Agriculture, Entrepreneurship and Ecotourism, Environmental Preservation
  • Group 3: Social Equity, Gender and Development, Good Health and Wellbeing

Abstract Format

The abstract should follow a similar format as the research category: a 150-300 word informative digest of objectives, methodology, and significant findings, submitted through the provided link.

Extension Paper Report Format

The extension paper should include the program/project title, faculty extensionists, duration, rationale, methodology, outcomes, and references.

PAPER PRESENTATION FORMAT

Only the study leader or authorized representative may present their extension project. Ten (10) minutes will be allocated for the pre-recorded presentation and another ten (10) minutes for the Q&A portion. Maximum of ten (10) slides only and the PowerPoint presentation should contain the following:

  • ➢ Program/Project Title, Faculty Extensionists, Duration, Cooperating Agency
  • ➢ Rationale (in bullet form)
  • ➢ Objectives
  • ➢ Methodology
  • ➢ Development Framework
  • ➢ Outcomes/Results of the Project (in bullet form)
  • ➢ Conclusion
  • ➢ Recommendation
  • ➢ Further Plan
  • ➢ References

E-Poster Presentation Guidelines

The e-poster must be in video format (Microsoft formats like .asf, .wmv, .mp4). It will be displayed in a poster loop online. Multimedia (picture/video) can be integrated, and the total duration must not exceed 3 minutes.

Each e-poster should contain category (research or extension), title, authors, affiliation, rationale, objectives, methodology, results, and conclusion. The layout should have a 16x9 aspect ratio and a minimum font size of 28.
